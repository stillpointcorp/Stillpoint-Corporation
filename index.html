Research Orientation

Stillpoint Corporation operates from the research-informed hypothesis that the sense of a separate, fixed self may be a cognitive construction rather than an ontological constant.
Contemporary findings in neuroscience suggest that self-referential identity correlates with activity in large-scale brain networks associated with narrative modelling and predictive inference (Raichle et al., 2001; Buckner & DiNicola, 2019).
Predictive processing frameworks further describe perception and identity as continuously generated models rather than static entities (Friston, 2010; Seth, 2013).
Parallel research in contemplative science demonstrates that alterations in self-perception can be measured through shifts in attentional training and neural activity (Brewer et al., 2011; Dahl et al., 2015).
Stillpoint Corporation does not advance doctrine. We apply structured conceptual inquiry to examine whether perceived separation reflects objective structure or cognitive interpretation.
Our work positions consciousness as a subject of disciplined investigation.
